About Taniti

Tahiti’s golden sands and turquoise waters offer a perfect escape. Relax under swaying palms or snorkel among vibrant marine life—an unforgettable tropical paradise awaits.

Taniti is a small, tropical island in the Pacific. While the island has an area of less than 500 square miles, the terrain is varied and includes both sandy and rocky beaches, a small but safe harbor, lush tropical rainforests, and a mountainous interior that includes a small, active volcano. Taniti has an indigenous population of about 20,000. Until a recent increase in tourism, most of the Tanitian economy was dominated by fishing or agriculture.

Experience the serene beauty of Tahiti's pristine beaches. With soft, golden sands, turquoise waters, and swaying palm trees, these idyllic shorelines offer the perfect escape for relaxation or adventure. Whether you're lounging by the water, snorkeling among vibrant marine life, or enjoying a romantic sunset, Tahiti's beaches promise an unforgettable tropical paradise.

Taniti is known for its stunning natural beauty, vibrant culture, and warm hospitality. Visitors can explore the island's pristine beaches, hike through lush rainforests, or visit the active volcano. Tanitians take pride in their traditions, and you’ll find plenty of local festivals and events showcasing music, dance, and cuisine.

Explore Tahiti’s lush rainforests, filled with vibrant greenery, colorful flowers, and serene trails. A haven of natural beauty and tranquility.

Adventure seekers can enjoy activities such as snorkeling, diving, and trekking. For those looking to relax, Taniti offers serene beaches with crystal-clear waters and world-class resorts. The combination of natural splendor and cultural richness makes Taniti a must-visit destination.

Discover Tahiti’s majestic volcanic landscapes, surrounded by lush greenery and scenic trails. A perfect blend of adventure and natural wonder.

Quick Facts About Taniti

  • Location: Pacific Ocean
  • Population: About 20,000
  • Coordinates: 15°S 145°W
  • Area: Less than 500 square miles
  • Known For: Pristine beaches, rainforests, and active volcano
  • Currency: U.S. dollars
  • Power Outlets: 120 volts (same as U.S.)
  • Language: English is widely spoken among younger locals
  • Climate: Warm and tropical year-round
